# Amplify X-Change Brand Notes

## Public Naming

- **Public brand name:** Amplify X-Change
- **Campaign/sub-brand:** Property Pathway
- **Byline:** by Amplify Solutions Group
- **Internal source key:** `asg_x_smsf` remains unchanged for now.

## Mini Mission Statement

Due to our success supporting in-person clients, Amplify X-Change is opening up
its property pathway education and discovery process to Australians nationwide —
combining local experience with a guided, digital-first experience.

The current pathway includes general information about SMSF property
considerations and connects users with the right professional support where
required.

## Colour Direction

- Dark navy / obsidian base: premium, quiet, editorial.
- Brass/gold accents: AX mark, divider lines, focus accents, CTA details.
- White/ivory typography: primary wordmark and high-contrast copy.
- Use brass sparingly so it reads as an accent, not a full-page colour wash.

## Logo Usage Notes

- Use `public/brand/amplify-xchange-wordmark.svg` for header, footer, and broad
  horizontal placements.
- Use `public/brand/ax-mark.svg` for decorative or compact brand moments where
  the full wordmark is already nearby.
- Use `public/brand/ax-icon.svg` for favicon/app icon contexts.
- Do not use the PNG concept boards directly as site logos. They are reference
  material only.
- Keep clear space around the AX mark and avoid placing it over busy imagery.
- Keep the public campaign line as `Property Pathway`, not the older SMSF
  campaign name.

## Favicon / App Icon Notes

- The active favicon is `/brand/ax-icon.svg`.
- The same SVG is referenced as the current app/touch icon for demo readiness.
- A production icon pack can later add PNG fallbacks if required by the final
  deployment target.

## Words To Avoid In Public Copy

- ATO loophole
- You qualify
- Approved
- Guaranteed returns
- Use your super now
- Best investment strategy
- Tax-free wealth
- Retire rich
- Elevate your wealth
- Strategic acquisition
- High-net-worth
- Financial levers
- Expected returns
- Likely returns

Prefer safer language such as "general information only", "may be worth
discussing", "illustrative only", "based on assumptions", and "speak with
licensed or appropriately qualified professionals".
