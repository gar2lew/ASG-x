# ASG-x Compliance Guardrails

## General-Information-Only Rules
- All content must be framed as general information only.
- No personal financial advice may be implied or given.
- Avoid language that suggests suitability for any individual.
- Do not make comparisons to other investment strategies as superior.
- All statements must be balanced, mentioning both potential benefits and risks/costs.
- NEEDS REVIEW: Final approval of all public-facing copy by compliance/legal.

## SMSF/Property Advice Boundary Notes
- Do not recommend establishing an SMSF.
- Do not recommend rolling over super into an SMSF.
- Do not suggest that an SMSF is suitable for any individual.
- Do not provide specific investment advice related to property or super.
- Do not discuss specific property investments or locations.
- Do not discuss lending strategies or borrowing within an SMSF.
- Do not discuss SMSF setup, administration, or compliance details.
- NEEDS REVIEW: Boundary definitions to be reviewed by compliance/legal.

## Referral/Commission Disclosure Requirements
- Disclose that ASG-x may receive referral fees from licensed professionals.
- Disclosure must be clear, prominent, and presented before consent is given.
- Disclosure must state that referral fees do not affect the cost to the user.
- Users must explicitly consent to sharing their information for referral purposes.
- NEEDS REVIEW: Exact disclosure wording and placement to be reviewed by compliance/legal.

## Consent Requirements
- Consent must be explicit, informed, and freely given.
- No pre-ticked boxes.
- Separate consent for each contact method (phone, SMS, email).
- Separate consent for sharing information with partners for referral purposes.
- Consent must include acknowledgment of privacy policy and referral disclosure.
- Consent version and timestamp must be recorded.
- IP address at time of consent must be logged for audit purposes.
- NEEDS REVIEW: Consent flow and wording to be reviewed by privacy/legal.

## Risk/Cost Disclosure Themes
- SMSF property investment involves risks, including potential loss of capital.
- SMSFs involve ongoing costs (accounting, audit, tax, legal, property management).
- Borrowing within an SMSF (limited recourse borrowing arrangements) carries additional risks.
- SMSF property may be illiquid and difficult to sell quickly.
- SMSFs require ongoing compliance with superannuation and tax laws.
- Diversification principles apply; concentrating super in one asset class increases risk.
- NEEDS REVIEW: Specific risk and cost language to be reviewed by compliance/legal.

- ❌ "You should set up an SMSF."
- ❌ "Rolling over your super is a good idea."
- ❌ "This strategy is perfect for your situation."
- ❌ "You will save tax by doing this."
- ❌ "This is a guaranteed way to grow your super."
- ❌ "I recommend this strategy."
- ❌ "You qualify for this strategy."
- ❌ "This is an ATO loophole."
- ❌ "You can access your super now."
- ❌ "This is the best investment strategy."
- ❌ "You'll make tax-free money."
- NEEDS REVIEW: Additional prohibited phrases based on ASIC RG 175 and RG 178.
