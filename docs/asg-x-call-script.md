# ASG-x Call Script

## Opening Script
"Good [morning/afternoon/evening], thank you for making time to speak with us today. My name is [Your Name] from ASG-x. Before we begin, I want to let you know that this call is for general information purposes only. We do not provide personal financial advice, and anything we discuss today should not be considered as a recommendation. Is it okay if I proceed with a few general questions to understand your current situation and what you're looking to explore?"

## Advice-Boundary Disclaimer (to be used early and reiterated as needed)
"Please remember that I am not a licensed financial adviser and cannot provide personal advice. The information I share is general in nature. For advice tailored to your specific situation, you would need to consult with a licensed financial adviser who considers your full financial circumstances, goals, and needs."

## Discovery Questions
1. "Can you tell me a little bit about what prompted your interest in exploring SMSF property strategies?"
2. "Do you currently have an SMSF, or are you considering setting one up?"
3. "What is your current approximate superannuation balance (if you're comfortable sharing)?"
4. "Are you looking to invest in residential property, commercial property, or are you still exploring your options?"
5. "What timeframe are you considering for making any decisions?"
6. "Are you currently speaking with any financial advisers, accountants, or mortgage brokers about your superannuation or property investment?"
7. "What are your main goals or objectives you're hoping to achieve by exploring this area?"
8. "Have you considered any potential risks or costs associated with SMSF property investment?"

## How to Explain the Pathway Safely
- Use hypothetical examples: "Some people consider using their super to invest in property through an SMSF because it allows them to potentially [state a general characteristic, e.g., 'hold property within a tax-advantaged structure']."
- Always follow with: "However, this approach involves significant complexity, costs, and compliance requirements. It's not suitable for everyone, and you would need to seek personalized advice to determine if it aligns with your situation."
- Never say: "This would work for you" or "You should do this."
- Always frame as: "This is one pathway that some people explore, but it requires careful consideration and professional guidance."

## How to Avoid Giving Personal Advice
- Redirect personal questions to general information: "I can't advise on your specific situation, but I can share general information about how the process typically works."
- Use qualifying language: "Some people consider...", "It's possible to...", "One approach that some individuals explore is..."
- Avoid words like: should, must, recommend, advise, suggest, suitable, ideal, perfect, guaranteed, certain, definite.
- Focus on process: "The typical steps involved in setting up an SMSF for property investment include..."
- Emphasize the need for professional advice: "Any decisions should be made after consulting with licensed professionals who consider your full picture."

## Referral Handoff Language
"If, based on our conversation, you'd like to explore speaking with a licensed professional about SMSF property strategies, and you provide your explicit consent, I can share your contact details with [type of professional, e.g., a financial adviser who specializes in SMSF strategies]. Would you be willing to provide consent for me to share your information for this purpose?"

## Closing Script
"Thank you for your time today. To recap, we've discussed the general process and considerations around SMSF property investment. Remember, I've provided general information only—any decisions about your superannuation should be made after consulting with licensed professionals who consider your individual circumstances.

If you'd like to proceed with a referral to a licensed professional, I'll need your explicit consent to share your information. Otherwise, you're welcome to reach out again if you have more general questions.

Is there anything else I can help clarify today?"
