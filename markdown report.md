# 2. Compliance and risk report

This is the part I would take very seriously.

## 2.1 SMSF/property advice boundary

ASIC says you must be authorised under an AFS licence to provide financial product advice. ASIC also says arranging for a person to apply for or acquire a financial product may constitute dealing unless exempt. ([ASIC][3])

ASIC’s SMSF guidance is even more direct: real property itself is not a financial product, but an **interest in an SMSF is a financial product**, and recommending or giving an opinion on using an SMSF to invest in real property is financial product advice. ([ASIC][4])

That means ASG-x must avoid staff or website copy saying things like:

- “You should set up an SMSF.”
- “This is the best way to use your super.”
- “You qualify to buy property with your super.”
- “This strategy is suitable for you.”
- “You should roll over your super.”
- “This property will make you X return.”
- “This is better than your current super fund.”

Instead, the site and staff should say:

- “You may be eligible to explore this pathway.”
- “A licensed adviser can assess whether this is suitable.”
- “We can connect you with appropriately licensed professionals.”
- “This information is general only.”
- “The next step is an education/discovery appointment, not advice.”

---

## 2.2 SMSF property rules to respect

MoneySmart says SMSF property must meet the **sole purpose test**, cannot be acquired from a related party of a member, and cannot be lived in or rented by a fund member or related party. Business premises have different rules and may be leased to a member if specific rules are followed and rent is at market rates. ([Moneysmart][5])

MoneySmart also lists costs including advice fees, SMSF setup, legal fees, stamp duty, ongoing SMSF accounting/audit/ATO/ASIC costs, property costs, loan costs, and insurance costs. ([Moneysmart][5])

Borrowing through an SMSF must follow strict rules and is usually done through a **limited recourse borrowing arrangement**, where the SMSF can only purchase a single asset such as one residential or commercial property. ([Moneysmart][5])

This should affect ASG-x’s funnel. The quiz should not only ask “how much super do you have?” It should screen for suitability and risk:

| Area                         | Example quiz field                           |
| ---------------------------- | -------------------------------------------- |
| Super balance                | Approximate combined super balance           |
| Age                          | Age range                                    |
| Employment                   | Employed, self-employed, retired, other      |
| Existing SMSF                | Yes/no                                       |
| Goal                         | Residential investment, commercial, not sure |
| Advice status                | Already have adviser/accountant?             |
| Timeframe                    | Now, 3 months, 6 months, research only       |
| Risk warning acknowledgement | Must tick                                    |
| Consent                      | Phone, SMS, email, partner referral consent  |

---

## 2.3 Referral and commission disclosure

EZISMSF discloses that it receives referral fees/commissions and that users will deal directly with third-party companies after submission. ([ezismsf.com][1])

ASG-x should be more polished:

- clear referral disclosure near the form
- partner disclosure before referral
- privacy collection notice
- consent to contact
- consent to share with named categories of partners
- no hidden “blanket consent”
- unsubscribe mechanism for marketing
- CRM audit log of consent version accepted

This is important because if ASG-x grows, the compliance issue will not just be what was said verbally — it will be what the website, forms, SMS messages, emails, and CRM notes prove happened.

This is a good idea, but the winning version is **not “copy EZISMSF.”**

The winning version is:

> Build **ASG-x** as a more credible, compliant, online-first SMSF/property enquiry and appointment funnel, backed by your CRM, Zoom process, Salestrail activity tracking, partner referral workflow, and strict advice-boundary controls.

I would move forward in this order:

1. **DeepSeek audit** using Prompt 1
2. **Antigravity blueprint** using Prompt 2
3. **Codex MVP build** using Prompt 3
